Paternity rights guide

A plain-English guide to paternity leave, Statutory Paternity Pay and the practical checks that matter most before relying on an employer figure.

At a glance

Paternity leave and paternity pay are related but separate checks. From 6 April 2026, eligible employees can take paternity leave from day one of employment, while Statutory Paternity Pay still has its own conditions.

Key points

  • Paternity leave and paternity pay do not follow exactly the same qualification rules.
  • From 6 April 2026, paternity leave can be available from the first day of employment.
  • Statutory Paternity Pay is usually the statutory weekly rate or 90% of average weekly earnings, whichever is lower.
  • Notice still matters, though some transitional rules apply around the April 2026 change.

What often causes confusion

  • People often assume that qualifying for leave automatically means qualifying for pay.
  • The notice rules differ depending on dates and whether the birth or placement falls around the April 2026 change.
  • Adoption and surrogacy routes can have different wording and timing rules.
  • Shared Parental Leave can affect how families plan time off.

Leave basics

Paternity leave is for employees who meet the relevant conditions and give the correct notice. From 6 April 2026, the service requirement for leave changes to day one, which is a significant practical change for workers who recently changed jobs.

Pay basics

Statutory Paternity Pay is usually paid at the statutory weekly rate or 90% of average weekly earnings, whichever is lower. Tax and National Insurance are normally deducted in the same way as wages.
