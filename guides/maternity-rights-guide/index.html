Maternity rights guide

A plain-English guide to maternity leave, Statutory Maternity Pay, holiday accrual, notice and the checks that matter most before relying on an employer figure.

At a glance

Employees can usually take up to 52 weeks of Statutory Maternity Leave. SMP can be paid for up to 39 weeks if the qualifying conditions are met, and holiday still builds up during maternity leave.

Key points

  • Statutory Maternity Leave is up to 52 weeks: 26 weeks of Ordinary Maternity Leave and 26 weeks of Additional Maternity Leave.
  • The earliest leave can usually start is 11 weeks before the expected week of childbirth.
  • You must usually take at least 2 weeks off after the birth, or 4 weeks if you work in a factory.
  • SMP is usually paid for up to 39 weeks if you qualify.

What often causes confusion

  • Leave and pay are separate questions: someone may qualify for leave but not SMP.
  • Holiday continues to accrue during maternity leave.
  • Contractual maternity pay can be more generous than the statutory minimum.
  • Return-to-work rights can differ depending on how much leave is taken and what changed during absence.

Leave basics

Statutory Maternity Leave is available to employees who give the correct notice. It does not depend on length of service, hours worked, or earnings in the same way SMP does.

Employment rights continue during maternity leave. That includes holiday accrual and the right to return to work.

Pay basics

SMP is paid for up to 39 weeks. The first 6 weeks are usually paid at 90% of average weekly earnings. The next 33 weeks are paid at the statutory weekly rate or 90% of average weekly earnings, whichever is lower.
