Settlement Agreement Tax Checker

What this checker helps with

Use it when you have a draft package or written breakdown and want a quicker read on which parts look like payroll items and which parts look closer to compensation.

It separates items that are usually taxed like earnings, such as PILON, holiday pay and wages, from amounts that may fall into the qualifying termination-payment category.

Important:

This is a first-check tool, not legal or tax advice. Use it to test the shape of the package before you sign, accept a headline figure, or assume payroll has treated every item correctly.

What this page covers

Statutory redundancy pay, enhanced severance or compensation-style amounts, PILON, holiday pay, and unpaid wages or bonus.

What this page does not cover

Personal tax advice, payroll calculations, PENP detail calculations, or every clause in a settlement agreement.

Before relying on the tax split
  • Separate payroll-style items from compensation-style items on paper first.
  • Do not treat the tax split as a verdict on whether the overall offer is fair.
  • Check the written breakdown, the agreement wording and whether independent advice costs are being covered.
Three fast checks on the written agreement
  • Make sure PILON, wages, bonus and holiday are listed separately from compensation-style sums.
  • Check whether statutory redundancy pay and any extra severance are described clearly.
  • Check who is paying for the independent adviser and whether the draft reflects the same labels used by payroll.

Review and rule focus

Last reviewed: 28 March 2026

Aim: help users test whether the written breakdown looks coherent before they rely on the headline number.

Core rule points used

  • The first combined £30,000 of certain qualifying termination-payment elements may be tax free.
  • PILON is generally taxable.
  • Holiday pay and unpaid wages are generally taxable as earnings.
  • A settlement agreement must meet legal conditions to be valid, including independent advice.
What to verify before you sign

Check exactly which amounts are described as PILON, holiday pay, wages, redundancy pay or compensation. Then check whether the written agreement and payroll treatment match that breakdown.
