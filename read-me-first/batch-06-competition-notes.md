# Batch 6 competition findings

## Main live patterns seen in competing pages
- Stronger redundancy pages often give richer next-step context, explain contractual redundancy, and surface practical outputs beyond the raw figure.
- Notice-period competitors often help users calculate an actual last day of work or end date, not just the legal minimum notice period.
- Holiday competitors often make pro-rata inputs easier to understand by asking about starters, leavers, or part-year coverage in a more natural way.
- Higher-value settlement pages often put trust signals front and centre, such as clear review dates, named legal review, and obvious category breakdowns.

## What Batch 6 changed because of that
- Redundancy calculator now handles the 6 April 2026 statutory-rate change and can show a 6-month claim-deadline guide date.
- Notice calculator now adds an estimated notice end date.
- Holiday calculator now uses a simpler months-covered input for regular-pattern pro-rata checks.
- Homepage trust signals were strengthened with review, source, and no-sign-up positioning.
