# Batch 49 competition notes

## Why this batch
The settlement gap was not missing content volume. It was clarity. The stronger solicitor-led competitors do three things better than average:
- they separate taxable payroll items from compensation-style amounts more explicitly
- they explain the £30,000 rule more clearly
- they tell users what to verify in the written agreement before relying on the offer

## What WorkRightsUK should do better
WorkRightsUK should stay less sales-driven than solicitor sites, but be better at:
- splitting the package into cleaner buckets
- routing mixed-package users into final pay or redundancy where appropriate
- keeping the explanation practical and source-backed without sounding like legal marketing

## Source grounding used for this batch
- GOV.UK termination-pay guidance for what a termination payment can include and how tax is deducted
- GOV.UK guidance on what is taxed as earnings versus what may sit inside the qualifying termination-payment rules
- Acas settlement-agreement guidance and template wording for validity and PILON treatment
