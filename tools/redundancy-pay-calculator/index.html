Statutory redundancy tool

Redundancy Pay Calculator

What this tool does

It estimates statutory redundancy pay using the current age bands, service rules and weekly-pay caps, then helps you check what else may still need to be looked at separately.

Use the dismissal date so the page can apply the right statutory cap. Then compare the result with notice pay, holiday owed and any enhanced contractual terms.

Important:

Statutory redundancy pay is often only one part of the picture. Notice pay, holiday owed, unpaid wages, bonuses, commission, benefits and enhanced contractual redundancy can all sit outside this estimate.

Before relying on the figure
  • Check whether your contract or staff handbook promises enhanced redundancy terms.
  • Ask for a written breakdown showing redundancy, notice, holiday and any deductions separately.
  • Check your dismissal date carefully because the statutory weekly cap changes on 6 April 2026.
  • Use the notice and final-pay pages next if your employer’s total figure looks like a mixed package.

What this estimate assumes

  • You normally need at least 2 years with the employer to qualify.
  • Service used for the statutory calculation is capped at 20 full years.
  • Average weekly pay is based on the 12 weeks before notice.
  • The page switches between the pre-6-April-2026 and from-6-April-2026 statutory caps.

What to check before relying on the figure

  • Your contract or staff handbook may offer enhanced redundancy.
  • Notice pay and holiday owed are separate checks.
  • Alternative-work offers, misconduct issues or exclusions can change entitlement.
  • You usually have 6 months from the job end date to claim unpaid statutory redundancy pay.
What the result means

Treat the figure as a statutory minimum check

This estimate shows the statutory redundancy minimum. Your final package may also include notice pay, holiday owed, unpaid wages, bonus, commission or enhanced contractual redundancy terms.

  • It is a minimum statutory redundancy estimate only.
  • It does not automatically include notice pay, holiday owed, unpaid wages, bonus or commission.
  • It can also be beaten by enhanced contractual redundancy terms.
What to ask next

Questions worth putting to HR or payroll straight away

  • Please break the package down into redundancy pay, notice pay, holiday pay and anything else.
  • Have you used the statutory minimum only, or an enhanced contractual redundancy policy?
  • What dismissal date and weekly pay figure did you use?
  • Is any notice being worked, paid in lieu, or offset against other sums?
Why figures differ

The most common reasons a real package comes out higher or lower

Differences often come from service length, the weekly pay cap, enhanced contract terms, exclusions, or the fact that employers are combining several end-of-employment payments into one offer.

Best linked follow-up

Most users need one more check after this

Use the Notice Period Calculator if the employer’s figure also includes notice, or go to Redundancy vs settlement vs final pay if you are still not sure what type of package you have been offered.

Deadline awareness

Do not leave the unpaid statutory figure unchecked for too long

If the statutory minimum appears unpaid or understated, use the guide date shown by the tool and check the official claim route promptly. Deadlines matter more than perfect certainty about every component.
