# Batch 32 competition notes

## Why this batch
Competitor unfair-dismissal pages often do two things better than generic employment-rights sites:
1. they give users a rough compensation shape, not just legal prose
2. they explain when the issue is really about dismissal fairness rather than payroll or settlement tax

WorkRightsUK had a visible public stub on this route, which was a trust problem.

## What this batch closes
- replaces the stub with a real estimator and source-backed framing
- gives the site a stronger answer to "what might unfair dismissal be worth?"
- improves internal routing from final-pay and compare pages so users can diagnose the right path faster

## What still matters next
- wording polish on any remaining AI-sounding helper text across deeper pages
- eventual live-site QA pass on all tool inputs and result states on mobile
- future guide support if unfair-dismissal traffic becomes material, but without turning the site into a broad legal encyclopedia
